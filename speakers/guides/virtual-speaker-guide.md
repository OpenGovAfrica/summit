### OpenGov Africa Summit 2026: Virtual Speaker Guide
#### Pre-Recorded Keynote + Live Q&A Format

This is the standard format for virtual keynote and headline sessions on the Virtual Hub. Recording the talk in advance removes connectivity risk from the highest-stakes part of the session, while live Q&A keeps the interaction real.

### 1. Session Format

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total slot | 30 minutes |
| Pre-recorded talk | 20 to 25 minutes |
| Live Q&A | 5 to 10 minutes |
| Speaker presence | Live for Q&A only (on camera, real time) |

### 2. What the Speaker Needs to Prepare

**Content**
- Final talk title and a 2 to 3 sentence abstract, for the schedule page and program
- Headshot and short bio
- Slides, if used (recommended for virtual: 8 to 12 slides, visual over text-heavy)

**Recording**
- Record in a quiet space with good lighting and a stable internet connection for upload (not for the live event itself)
- Speak to camera at eye level, not down at a laptop
- Keep the recording within the agreed 20 to 25 minute window
- Deliver the raw file, our team (or the speaker) can do light editing if needed

**Live portion**
- Confirm the platform and how they will join for the Q&A segment
- Decide in advance: moderator-relayed questions (pulled from chat and submissions) or open floor
- Have a backup contact method (phone number, alternate email) in case of a dropped connection at the last minute

### 3. Timeline for the Speaker

| Milestone | Timing |
|---|---|
| Title, abstract, bio, headshot due | 3 to 4 weeks before summit |
| Slides due (if using) | 2 weeks before summit |
| Recording due | 1 week before summit |
| Review and approval of recording | 3 to 4 days before summit |
| Live tech check for Q&A segment | 1 to 2 days before summit |
| Live Q&A | Day of session |

### 4. What Our Team Handles

- Confirm streaming platform and share joining link for the live Q&A portion
- Upload and queue the pre-recorded talk for playback
- Assign a moderator to run the transition from recording to live Q&A and to relay questions if needed
- Recording consent and permissions for post-summit distribution
- On-screen partnership or sponsor credit, if applicable (lower third, intro slide, or verbal mention by moderator)
- Backup plan: if a speaker cannot join live at all, moderator can read out a few pre-submitted answers or the session can close with the recording alone

### 5. Email Checklist (for outreach team)

When confirming a virtual speaker, the confirmation email should cover:
- [ ] Session format explained (pre-recorded talk plus live Q&A)
- [ ] Length confirmed (20 to 25 min recording, 5 to 10 min live Q&A)
- [ ] Deadlines for title, abstract, bio, headshot, slides, and recording
- [ ] Recording guidance (space, lighting, camera framing, file format)
- [ ] Platform and joining instructions for the live portion
- [ ] Tech check scheduled 1 to 2 days prior
- [ ] Q&A format confirmed (moderated vs open)
- [ ] Recording consent for post-summit use
- [ ] Partnership or sponsor credit language, if relevant

*This guide applies to all virtual keynote and headline sessions across the five summit days. For in-person Physical Hub sessions, live delivery remains the default format.*
