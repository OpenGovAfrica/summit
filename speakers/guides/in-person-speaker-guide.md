### OpenGov Africa Summit 2026: In-Person Speaker Guide
#### Physical Hub, Lagos

This is the standard format for speakers presenting live at the Physical Hub. Sessions are delivered in real time to both the in-room audience and the Global Virtual Hub livestream.

### Session format

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total slot | 30 to 45 minutes, depending on track |
| Live talk | 20 to 30 minutes |
| Live Q&A | 10 to 15 minutes |
| Speaker presence | Live, in-room, for the full session |

### What the speaker needs to prepare

**Content**
- Final talk title and a 2 to 3 sentence abstract, for the schedule page and program
- Headshot and short bio
- Slides, if used (8 to 15 slides is typical, sent in advance for AV loading)

**Travel and logistics**
- Confirm arrival date and whether the summit is covering or coordinating travel and accommodation
- Visa support letter, if needed (via the Google Form intake)
- Confirm on-site contact for the day of the session with summit director (especially for Keynote Speakers)

**Delivery**
- Arrive at the venue with enough buffer for a sound and slide check before the session
- Bring a backup of slides on a USB drive in addition to the version sent in advance
- All talks will be recorded for post-summit distribution

**Live portion**
- Q&A is live in-room, with a moderator managing time and, where relevant, folding in questions submitted by the Virtual Campus audience
- Speaker should expect a small number of virtual questions to be read out by the moderator

### Timeline for the speaker

| Milestone | Timing |
|---|---|
| Title, abstract, bio, headshot due | 3 to 4 weeks before summit |
| Slides due | 1 week before summit |
| Travel and visa details confirmed | 3 to 4 weeks before summit |
| Arrival and venue walkthrough | Day before session, where possible |
| Sound and slide check | Day of session, before doors open |

### What our team handles

- Venue AV setup and slide loading
- Moderator assigned to manage timing and Q&A, including questions relayed from the Virtual Campus
- Recording and livestream setup
- On-site signage and speaker introduction
- Recording consent and permissions for post-summit distribution
- On-screen or verbal partnership or sponsor credit, if applicable

### Email checklist (for outreach team)

When confirming an in-person speaker, the confirmation email should cover:
- [ ] Session format explained (live talk plus live Q&A)
- [ ] Length confirmed (20 to 30 min talk, 10 to 15 min Q&A)
- [ ] Deadlines for title, abstract, bio, headshot, and slides
- [ ] Travel and accommodation arrangement (covered, coordinated, or self-managed)
- [ ] Visa support letter offered, if needed
- [ ] Arrival date and venue walkthrough timing
- [ ] Sound and slide check scheduled for day of session
- [ ] Recording consent for post-summit use
- [ ] Partnership or sponsor credit language, if relevant

*This guide applies to all in-person sessions at the Physical Hub across the five summit days. For virtual sessions, the default format is a pre-recorded talk with live Q&A. See `virtual-speaker-guide.md`.*
